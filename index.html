IoT and botnets

Published: 10/10/2024

Author: Evan Hoile

There are a lot of threats that exist on the internet, but botnets are one of the scarier ones. The term botnet describes a network of hardware that has been infected. Once infected the victim's device can be controlled remotely. IoT botnets are botnets that specifically target vulnerable IoT devices such as ring cameras to rapidly increase computing power. Botnets are commonly used for denial of service attacks whether it's in enterprise or not. A DoS attack is simply flooding a target with packets in order to block any traffic getting through. The power behind DDoS attacks come from botnets because of the immense power required. This can be considered a huge threat to businesses for just being able to take a system offline. Botnets are constantly growing and gaining more computing power. Since infected devices are able to execute code remotely malware and other software is able to be installed without users noticing. This threat could be devastating for businesses if little or no security measures are in place. Just one infected device could mean the installation of ransomware, malware, and other threatening software to the whole network. While many threats to enterprises exist today IoT botnets seem to be the most versatile. From DDoS attacks to the installation of other malware, businesses need to be aware of threats and take preventative measures to keep operating.
